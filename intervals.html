Intervals

An interval is the distance betwwen two notes. Unison, fourths, fifths, and octaves are perfect intervals when in their fundamental form.

Raising the top tone of a perfect interval changes it to an augmented interval. Lowering the top tone changes it to a diminished interval.

Non-perfect diatonic intervals are major in their fundamental form. When lowered, they become minor, and when raised they become augmented. Lowering a minor interval makes it a diminished interval.

Perfect intervals:


Diminished - Perfect - Augmented

Major and minor intervals:


Diminished - minor - major - Augmented

Abbreviations:


P = Perfect
m = minor
M = Major
D = Diminished
A = Augmented
